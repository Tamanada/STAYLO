## Minute-by-Minute Run Sheet

### Minute 0–1 | Greeting + Context Setting

**Action:** Hand over printed one-pager (TH side facing up). Wai if she initiates.

**Say (EN):**
> "Good afternoon, Khun Sasiwimol. Thank you for your time today. My name is David. I am building a booking platform for hoteliers — and I am starting here, on Koh Phangan."

**Phonetic key phrase:** Use **P1** on entry.

**Cultural note:** Use "Khun" before her first name at all times — never just "Sasiwimol" alone. If she offers a card, receive it with both hands, look at it briefly before putting it face-up on the table (not in your pocket immediately).

---

### Minute 1–2 | The Problem — OTA Commissions

**Action:** Point to the left column of the one-pager ("ปัญหา"). Do not read it aloud — let her glance.

**Say (EN):**
> "Today, every hotel on this island pays Booking.com between fifteen and eighteen percent. Every reservation. That money leaves the island. It does not come back."

**Phonetic key phrase:** Deliver **P2** with the printed figure visible.

**Tip:** Pause on the **$6.3M** figure. Let it land. No need to fill the silence.

---

### Minute 2–4 | The Solution — 10% + Cooperative Model

**Action:** Point to middle column ("วิธีแก้ปัญหา") and the commission comparison table.

**Say (EN):**
> "Staylo is different. Our commission is ten percent — and it is locked. It cannot be changed without a ninety-percent vote of all member hoteliers. You are not a customer. You are a co-owner."

**Phonetic key phrases:** Deliver **P3** then **P4**. Deliver them slowly — one sentence, pause, next sentence.

**Tip:** If she asks "what is a cooperative?" — simple answer: "Like a rice cooperative, but for hotel bookings. The hoteliers are the members and they own it together."  
Thai shortcut: **"เหมือนสหกรณ์ครับ แต่เป็นสหกรณ์จองโรงแรม"**  
Phonetic: **"MEUAN sa-ha-GORN KRAP, dtae pen sa-ha-GORN JONG RONG-RAEM."**

---

### Minute 4–6 | Live Demo (if device available)

**Action:** Open staylo.app or the hotelier dashboard. Show briefly — do not go deep.

**Say (EN):**
> "This is the platform today. A hotelier manages their listings, tracks bookings, and in month seven, earns $STAY tokens — ten tokens per night — which they can use to reduce their commission further, to five percent."

**Note:** Avoid the word "crypto" in this minute. Say "tokens" or "digital rewards." She can ask about it — then you explain. Don't lead with it.

**If no device — skip to Minute 6.**

---

### Minute 6–7 | Transition to Co-ownership Pitch

**Action:** Lean forward slightly. Close the laptop or set the device aside. Make it personal.

**Say (EN):**
> "I am not asking you to sign a contract with us. I am inviting you to co-build this with us. Every hotel that joins becomes a part-owner. One vote per property — regardless of size. If you have a ten-room bungalow or a two-hundred-room resort, your vote counts the same."

**Phonetic key phrase:** Deliver **P5** in Thai. Slowly.

---

### Minute 7–8 | The Founding Partner Offer

**Action:** Point to right column ("ข้อเสนอ"). Tap the CTA box showing $1,000.

**Say (EN):**
> "The Founding Partner share is one thousand US dollars — one share, one vote, ten percent commission for life. The first three hundred hotels on Koh Phangan are eligible (three thousand shares total, ten shares max per property). After that, the price rises to fifteen hundred."
> 
> "There is no obligation today. But I wanted you to be the first to know, because your association represents the hotels who should own this platform."

**Tip:** The phrase "your association represents the hotels who should own this platform" is the most important sentence of the meeting. Deliver it slowly and directly.

---

### Minute 8–9 | Close + Invitation

**Action:** Slide the one-pager to her side of the table. Make eye contact.

**Say (EN):**
> "Koh Phangan is where we start. We need five hundred hotels registered across Thailand before the platform goes live — and three hundred of them should be from this island. Are you interested in being part of this?"

**Phonetic key phrase:** Deliver **P6**. Then: silence. Let her answer.

**If positive:** "Wonderful. I will send you the full document by email. My contact is on the bottom of that page." Hand her your card.

**If hesitant / "need more time":** "Of course. There is no pressure. I will follow up with you by email. May I have your contact?"

**If she asks about the $STAY token / crypto more:**  
> "The token is a reward system built into the platform. Hoteliers earn tokens for activity — nights booked, reviews, referrals. They can use them to reduce their commission. It is optional. You never have to touch it if you prefer not to."

---

### Minute 9–10 | Closing + Gratitude

**Action:** Stand when she stands. Wai if she initiates (match her level — do not initiate deeper than she does).

**Say (EN):**
> "Thank you very much, Khun Sasiwimol. It is a real honor. I hope we can build something meaningful for the hoteliers of this island together."

**Phonetic key phrase:** Deliver **P7** in Thai on exit.

**Leave behind:** The printed one-pager. Your business card (or write your email on the one-pager footer).

## Cultural Checklist

- Wai: hands together at chest level, slight bow. If Sasiwimol wais first, reciprocate at same level or slightly lower. Do not initiate a wai higher than her rank.
- Business card: receive with both hands. Place face-up on table. Do not write on it.
- Hierarchy: as president of the association, she holds significant social status. Express respect consistently. Never interrupt.
- Silence: Thai communication often uses silence as positive processing time. Do not fill it with words.
- Avoid: do not cross your legs toward her, do not point with your foot, do not touch the top of anyone's head.
- Dress: conservative. Long trousers, collared shirt minimum. No flip-flops.
